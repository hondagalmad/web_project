Ceres

Basic Stats


Type: Dwarf Planet / Asteroid

Diameter: ~940 km

Mass: ~0.00015× Earth’s mass

Moons: None

Length of day: ≈ 9 hours

Length of year: ≈ 4.6 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~414 million km (average)

Average temperature: ≈ −105°C

Composition: Rock, water ice, and hydrated minerals

Surface & Features


Ceres has a rocky surface mixed with water ice and clay minerals. It shows bright spots in craters, believed to be salts left from briny water beneath the surface. Ceres has no significant atmosphere, and its surface is heavily cratered. It may contain a subsurface ocean of liquid water, making it a subject of interest for planetary science.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

NASA’s Dawn spacecraft visited Ceres in 2015-2018. It mapped the surface, studied bright spots, examined craters and mountains, and collected detailed data on its composition and geology.

Upcoming Missions

Currently, no new missions to Ceres are officially planned, but scientists continue to study it using telescopes and hope for future exploration.

Weird Facts

  • Ceres is the largest object in the asteroid belt between Mars and Jupiter.
  • It was the first dwarf planet visited by a spacecraft (Dawn).
  • Bright spots in craters are likely deposits of sodium carbonate.
  • Ceres might have a subsurface ocean of liquid water.
  • It rotates roughly every 9 hours.
  • Its surface is a mix of rock and ice, with evidence of past cryovolcanism.
  • Ceres has no moons and a very thin exosphere.
