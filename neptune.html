Neptune

Basic Stats


Type: Ice giant

Diameter (equatorial): ~49,528 km

Mass: ~17.1 × Earth’s mass

Moons: 14 (known) + some smaller satellites

Length of day: ≈ 16.11 hours

Length of year: ≈ 164.8 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~4.5 billion km (≈ 30.1 AU)

Average cloud-top temperature: ~−200 °C

Aatmosphere & Surface Hell


Neptune’s atmosphere is mostly hydrogen, helium, and methane, which gives it its blue color. It has fast winds and layered clouds made of methane ice. Neptune has no solid surface — anything falling in would sink through dense gas and liquid layers toward a hot, high-pressure interior around a small core.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

The only spacecraft to visit Neptune up close was NASA’s Voyager 2 in 1989, which provided the first detailed images of the planet, its rings, and its moons There are currently no active spacecraft at Neptune, but modern telescopes such as the James Webb Space Telescope continue to observe the planet from afar, providing updated data on its atmosphere and rings

Upcoming Missions

Upcoming Missions: Proposed missions to Neptune include Neptune Odyssey — an orbiter plus atmospheric-probe mission aimed at studying Neptune, its rings, and its moons (including Triton), and Trident — a flyby mission targeting Neptune and its moon Triton. There are also other mission concepts being discussed by scientists, such as dual-orbiter ideas or long-term observation missions. All of these are currently in proposal or study stages and none have received formal approval or funding yet

Weird Facts

  • Neptune has the fastest winds in the solar system, reaching up to 2,100 km/h.
  • The planet has a dark spot similar to Jupiter’s Great Red Spot, but it appears and disappears over time.
  • Neptune emits more heat than it receives from the Sun, a mystery scientists are still studying.
  • A day on Neptune is very short: about 16 hours, even though the planet is very far from the Sun.
  • Neptune has rings that are faint and clumpy, unlike Saturn’s smooth and bright rings.
  • The moon Triton orbits Neptune backward, a phenomenon called retrograde orbit.
  • Neptune’s atmosphere has giant storms with clouds made of methane ice crystals.
