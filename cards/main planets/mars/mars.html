Mars

Basic Stats


Type: Terrestrial

Diameter: ~6,779 km

Mass: ~0.107× Earth’s mass

Moons: 2 known moons

Length of day: ≈ 24.6 hours

Length of year: ≈ 687 Earth days

Distance from Sun: ~225 million km (varies)

Average temperature: ≈ −63°C

Composition: Rock, iron, and thin CO₂ atmosphere

Atmosphere & Surface


Mars has a thin atmosphere dominated by carbon dioxide with traces of nitrogen and argon. Its surface is rocky with large volcanoes, deep valleys, and dusty plains that give it a red color. The planet has polar ice caps made of water and CO₂ frost above a rocky crust and regolith. Mars experiences seasons due to its axial tilt, and dust storms can sometimes envelop the whole planet.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

Many spacecraft have visited Mars: orbiters, landers, and rovers from NASA, ESA, India, and others. These missions mapped the surface, analyzed rock samples, studied the atmosphere, and searched for signs of past water and habitability.

Upcoming Missions

Future missions plan to return samples to Earth, send new rovers, and test technologies for human exploration. International and commercial programs aim for more detailed study in the 2020s and 2030s.

Weird Facts

  • Mars has the tallest volcano in the Solar System, Olympus Mons, over 21 km high.
  • It hosts the largest canyon, Valles Marineris, stretching thousands of kilometers.
  • The planet’s red color comes from iron oxide (rust) on its surface.
  • Dust devils and global dust storms reshape the surface over time.
  • Seasonal flows and recurring slope lineae are still under study for water signs.
  • Temperatures can vary drastically between day and night.
  • Many missions are focused on sample return and eventual human visits.
