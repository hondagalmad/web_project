Jupiter

Basic Stats


Type: Gas Giant

Diameter: ~139,820 km

Mass: ~317.8× Earth’s mass

Moons: 95 known moons

Length of day: ≈ 9.9 hours

Length of year: ≈ 11.86 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~778 million km

Average temperature: ≈ −108°C

Composition: Hydrogen and helium

Atmosphere & Surface


Jupiter is a massive gas giant with a thick atmosphere of hydrogen and helium. It features colorful bands, storms, and the Great Red Spot. The planet has a faint ring system and no solid surface.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

Notable missions include Pioneer, Voyager, Galileo, and Juno, studying the atmosphere, rings, magnetic field, and moons.

Upcoming Missions

Future missions aim to study Jupiter’s moons more closely and explore its magnetic environment.

Weird Facts

  • Great Red Spot is a storm larger than Earth.
  • Fast rotation causes a noticeable equatorial bulge.
  • Emits more heat than it receives from the Sun.
  • Has dozens of moons with diverse geology.
  • Has faint but complex ring system.
  • Atmosphere contains hydrogen, helium, methane, and ammonia clouds.
  • Strong magnetic field and intense radiation belts.
