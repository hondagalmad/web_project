Makemake

Basic Stats


Type: Dwarf Planet

Diameter: ~1,434 km

Mass: ~0.00066× Earth’s mass

Moons: 1 known moon

Length of day: ≈ 7.77 hours

Length of year: ≈ 309 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~6.85 billion km

Average temperature: ≈ −239°C

Composition: Rock and methane ice

Surface & Features


Makemake has a bright surface coated with methane ice. It has one known moon and no significant atmosphere. The surface is extremely cold and highly reflective in the Kuiper belt.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

No spacecraft have visited Makemake. Observations are conducted using ground-based and space telescopes.

Upcoming Missions

No official missions are planned yet, but astronomers continue monitoring its orbit and surface.

Weird Facts

  • One of the largest dwarf planets in the Kuiper belt.
  • Surface is highly reflective due to methane ice.
  • Has one known moon discovered in 2016.
  • Extremely low temperatures.
  • Orbits the Sun every 309 Earth years.
  • Has no significant atmosphere.
  • Discovered in 2005, recognized as dwarf planet in 2008.
