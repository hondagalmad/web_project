Haumea

Basic Stats


Type: Dwarf Planet

Diameter: ~1,632 km

Mass: ~0.0007× Earth’s mass

Moons: 2 known moons

Length of day: ≈ 4 hours

Length of year: ≈ 284 Earth years

Distance from Sun: ~6.4 billion km

Average temperature: ≈ −241°C

Composition: Rock, water ice

Surface & Features


Haumea has a fast rotation and an elongated shape. Its surface is mainly water ice, giving it a bright appearance. The two small moons, Hi'iaka and Namaka, orbit around it. Haumea is part of the Kuiper belt and has a very icy exterior.

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Missions

Haumea has not been visited by any spacecraft. Observations are made via ground-based and space telescopes.

Upcoming Missions

No official missions planned. Astronomers continue to study its unique shape, moons, and rapid rotation.

Weird Facts

  • Haumea is one of the fastest rotating large objects in the solar system.
  • Its rotation makes it elongated rather than spherical.
  • It has two small moons: Hi'iaka and Namaka.
  • Located in the Kuiper belt beyond Neptune.
  • Very icy and bright surface.
  • Extremely low gravity compared to Earth.
  • Discovered in 2004, officially recognized in 2008.
