DATA LOG: CLASSIFICATIONS

Analyzing key differences between celestial bodies.

Main Planets

  • Orbit the Sun directly.
  • Have sufficient mass to assume a nearly round shape.
  • CRITICAL: Have "cleared their neighborhood" of other objects. They are gravitationally dominant.
  • Example: Earth, Jupiter.

Dwarf Planets

  • Orbit the Sun directly.
  • Have sufficient mass to assume a nearly round shape.
  • CRITICAL: Have NOT cleared their neighborhood. They share their orbit with other debris.
  • Example: Pluto, Eris.
