Earth

Basic Stats


Type: Terrestrial planet (rocky)

Diameter (equatorial): ~12,756 km

Mass: ~5.97 × 10²⁴ kg

Moons: 1 (The Moon)

Length of day: ≈ 23.9 hours

Length of year: ≈ 365.25 days

Distance from Sun: ~150 million km (≈ 1 AU)

Average surface temperature: ~15 °C

Aatmosphere & Surface


Earth’s surface is divided into oceans, continents, mountains, deserts, forests, and icy regions. About 71% of the surface is covered by water, while the land includes tectonic plates that move slowly and shape Earth’s geography over time

Exploration & Missions

Past & Current Earth Observation Missions

Earth has been studied through a long series of observation satellites such as Landsat, Terra, Aqua, Aura, and the Sentinel series. These missions continuously monitor Earth’s atmosphere, land, oceans, and climate, providing essential data on weather patterns, vegetation, sea levels, and environmental changes. Together, they form the foundation of modern Earth science and climate research

Upcoming Earth Science & Observation Missions

Future Earth-observation missions include the joint NASA–ISRO satellite NISAR, designed to study land deformation and ecosystem changes, and ESA’s EarthCARE mission, which will explore cloud–aerosol interactions. Additional missions, such as new Sentinel satellites and advanced climate-monitoring programs, are under development to provide higher accuracy data for understanding Earth’s evolving climate and improving disaster prediction

Weird Facts

  • Earth is the only planet known to have liquid water on the surface, yet most of it is unexplored and deeper than any point humans have reached.
  • The Earth’s core is as hot as the surface of the Sun, reaching temperatures around 5,400°C
  • Earth’s rotation is gradually slowing down — days are getting longer by about 1.7 milliseconds per century
  • The Moon is drifting away from Earth at about 3.8 cm per year, slowly changing Earth’s tides and rotation
  • Earth’s magnetic poles flip every few hundred thousand years — the next flip is overdue
  • Only about 5% of Earth’s oceans have been explored; most of the planet is still unknown territory
  • Earth hums with a constant low-frequency vibration that humans cannot hear — the cause is still debated by scientists
